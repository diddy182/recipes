New England Clam Chowder for Two

A creamy New England clam chowder with bacon, Yukon Gold potatoes, and chopped clams, thickened by pureeing some of the solids. Finished with parsley and lemon.

Yield2 servings
Prep15 mins
Cook35 mins
Total50 mins

Ingredients

  • 2 strips of bacon (diced)
  • 2 tablespoons butter
  • 1 medium onion (large dice)
  • 2 stalks of celery (large dice)
  • 2 medium Yukon Gold potatoes (bite-size pieces)
  • 2 teaspoons garlic (minced)
  • ¼ cup white wine
  • 1 cup (8 oz) clam juice
  • 1 cup heavy cream
  • 1 tablespoon Worcestershire sauce
  • 1 teaspoon hot sauce
  • 2 bay leaves
  • 2 (6.5 ounce) cans chopped clams (save the juice)
  • ¼ cup fresh parsley (chopped)
  • Salt and pepper
  • Lemon wedges

Method

  1. In a large pot, cook the diced bacon over medium heat until crispy.
  2. Add the butter, onions, and celery. Season with salt and pepper and cook until the onions are translucent and the celery has softened, about 5–6 minutes.
  3. Add the potatoes, season with salt, and cook for another 3–4 minutes. Add the garlic and cook for another 1–2 minutes.
  4. Add the white wine, clam juice, heavy cream, Worcestershire, hot sauce, and bay leaves. Stir to combine and simmer for at least 20 minutes.
  5. Add the clams with their juice.
  6. With a slotted spoon, scoop out some of the solids and puree in a blender, then return the puree to the pot to thicken the chowder.
  7. Add the parsley and season with salt and pepper if needed. Garnish with lemon wedges. Enjoy!
